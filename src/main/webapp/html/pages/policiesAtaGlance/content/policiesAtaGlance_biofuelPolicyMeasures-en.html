Biofuels policies-detailed

Biofuel policies are classified into seven policy types. Each of these policy types consists of a set of policy measures. The organizational diagram illustrates this classification.

Select the biofuel policy type for which you would like to see more information. The graphs below show how many of the AMIS countries are implementing the selected biofuel policy type, disaggregated by the associated policy measures.

Note: The AMIS policy database records biofuel policies applied during the period starting from 2011 (for some countries information prior to 2011 is provided).

Frequency of biofuel policies - disaggregated by policy measure

Policy Type



The frequency graph shows how many AMIS countries have implemented the selected biofuel policy type, disaggregated by biofuel policy measures, for the selected period. A distinction is made between policies that are targeted on ethanol, biodiesel, or biofuel (unspecified). This last category is used when the legal document does not specify whether the policy applies to ethanol or biodiesel.
Note that policies are displayed at country-level, which means that if a certain country implements biofuel policies at different state-levels for the selected period, this country is only counted once. Individual policy measures can be removed from the graph by deselecting them from the legend in the graph.


Time series of biofuel policies - disaggregated by policy measure
Policy Type



This time series graph shows the evolution over time of how many AMIS countries have implemented the selected biofuel policy type, disaggregated by biofuel policy measures. A distinction is made between policies that are targeted on ethanol, biodiesel, or biofuel (unspecified). This last category is used when the legal document does not specify whether the policy applies to ethanol or biodiesel. The final tab, labelled “All”, combines all categories of biofuels. In this last graph, a country with a policy on ethanol and on biodiesel in a particular time period will only be counted once.
Policies are displayed at country-level, which means that if a certain country implements biofuel policies at different state-levels for the selected period, this country is only counted once. Policies without an explicit end date are shown to stay valid until the last date displayed in the graph. Individual policy measures can be removed from the graph by deselecting them from the legend in the graph.
