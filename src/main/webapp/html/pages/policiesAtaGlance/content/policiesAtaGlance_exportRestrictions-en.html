Export Restrictions

The AMIS policy database records information on 9 different export restrictive measures. The graphs below focus on 5 of these measures and show many of the AMIS countries are implementing these measures on the 4 AMIS crops (wheat, rice, maize and soybeans).

Note: The AMIS policy database records export restrictions applied during the period starting from 2007 (for some countries information prior to 2007 is provided).


Frequency of export restriction policies - disaggregated by policy measure




The frequency graph shows how many AMIS countries have implemented export restrictive measures during the selected period. A distinction is made between policies that are targeted on wheat, maize, rice and soybeans.
Note that policies are displayed at commodity class level, which means that if a certain country implements an export tax on two different types of wheat during the selected period, this country is only counted once in the wheat category.
Information on licensing requirements has been collected from 2007 onwards. Hence licensing requirements that were active before 2007 are not reported. Individual policy measures can be removed from the graph by deselecting them from the legend in the graph.


Time series of export restriction policies - disaggregated by policy measure


This time series graph shows the evolution over time of how many AMIS countries have implemented export restrictive measures. A distinction is made between policies that are targeted on wheat, maize, rice and soybeans.
Note that policies are displayed at commodity class level, which means that if a certain country implements an export tax on two different types of wheat during the selected period, this country is only counted once in the wheat category.
The final tab, labelled “All”, combines all commodity classes. In this last graph, a country with a policy on wheat and maize in a particular time period will only be counted once. Policies without an explicit end date are shown to stay valid until the last date displayed in the graph. Information on licensing requirements has been collected from 2007 onwards. Hence licensing requirements that were active before 2007 are not reported. Individual policy measures can be removed from the graph by deselecting them from the legend in the graph.
